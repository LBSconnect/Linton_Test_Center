Part 1

Standard Notary Procedures

Applies to all walk-in and scheduled notary appointments. All notarizations must comply with Texas Government Code, Chapter 406. This SOP does not cover remote online notarization (RON).

1

Supplies & Setup — Before Your Shift Opens

Daily Checklist

  • Notary seal/stamp — confirm ink is fresh and impression is clear
  • Notary journal — open to the next blank entry, pen available (blue or black ink only)
  • Certificate forms — acknowledgment, jurat, and copy certification blanks stocked
  • Review today's scheduled appointments in the booking system
  • Confirm scanner and printer are powered on and have paper/ink
  • Test the stamp on scratch paper — legible seal = good to go
⚠
Stamp ClarityAll text in the notary seal impression must be legible. A smudged or partial stamp can render the notarization invalid. Replace ink pads before they fade.
2

Customer Check-In

  1. 1
    Greet the customer and confirm their name and appointment time.
  2. 2
    Ask them to provide all documents requiring notarization — collect every page now so you can review them together.
  3. 3
    Direct them to the signing station and ask them to keep the documents unsigned until instructed.
  4. 4
    Log the appointment start time in the journal.
✕
Never begin while the customer is standing at the counter. The identity check and document review must be completed at a dedicated signing area with privacy from other customers.
3

Identity Verification

Texas law requires satisfactory identification of every signer. Each person signing must present one of the following:

Driver's License
State ID Card
U.S. Passport
U.S. Passport Card
Military ID (CAC)
Permanent Resident Card
Foreign Passport

Inspect Each ID for All Four Criteria

  1. A
    Photo — the person in front of you matches the photo. If uncertain, ask a second staff member to confirm.
  2. B
    Name — name on ID matches name on the document (minor variations like middle initial omission are acceptable; entirely different surnames are not).
  3. C
    Expiration — ID must not be expired on the date of notarization. A driver's license grace period does not apply for notary purposes.
  4. D
    Integrity — no visible laminate bubbles, altered text, or printed-over fields. If any signs of tampering are present, decline.
ℹ
Personal Knowledge If you personally know the signer well enough to vouch for their identity, Texas law permits personal knowledge as an alternative — document this explicitly in the journal.
✕
If ID fails any check — politely explain you are unable to proceed. Do not attempt to notarize. Offer them the opportunity to return with acceptable ID. Record the attempt and refusal in the journal.
4

Document Review — Before the Signer Signs Anything

  1. 1
    Flip through every page. Look for blank spaces that appear to require completion (dates, names, dollar amounts). A document with unfilled blanks may not be notarized.
  2. 2
    Locate the notary certificate. If one is already printed on the document, read the certificate type (acknowledgment or jurat). If none is present, you will attach a loose certificate after the act.
  3. 3
    Confirm the number of pages and signers matches what was booked.
  4. 4
    Do not read the body of the document for content. You are verifying structure, not advising on the substance.
⚠
Certificate Type — Do Not Choose for the Signer If a customer asks "which one should I use?", explain: an acknowledgment is for confirming a signature was made voluntarily; a jurat is for swearing to the truth of the contents. Direct them to their attorney or the receiving party for the final decision. Under no circumstances advise which certificate is legally appropriate for their situation.
5

The Notarial Act

Acknowledgment

  1. 1
    Ask: "Did you sign this document voluntarily and for its stated purposes?" The signer must confirm verbally.
  2. 2
    The signer does not need to sign in your presence for an acknowledgment — only acknowledge they signed it.
  3. 3
    If the document is unsigned, have them sign it now before you affix the certificate.

Jurat (Oath or Affirmation)

  1. 1
    The signer must not have signed yet. If they have, you cannot proceed with a jurat on that signature.
  2. 2
    Administer the oath: "Do you solemnly swear (or affirm) that the contents of this document are true and correct to the best of your knowledge?"
  3. 3
    The signer signs in your physical presence immediately after the oath.

Copy Certification

  1. 1
    You must have access to the original document. You cannot certify a copy of a copy.
  2. 2
    Make the copy yourself using the office scanner/copier and verify page-by-page that the copy is a true and complete reproduction.
  3. 3
    You may not certify copies of vital records (birth, death, marriage certificates) — those must be obtained from the issuing authority.
✓
Maintain Visual Contact Keep the signing signer, their ID, and the document in your field of view throughout the act. Never leave the room or look away during a jurat signing.
6

Certificate Completion

Complete every field. An incomplete certificate is as invalid as no certificate at all.

  1. 1
    Date: The date the notarial act occurred — never pre-date or post-date.
  2. 2
    Venue: Houston, Harris County, Texas (the location where the act is performed).
  3. 3
    Signer's Name: Write exactly as it appears on the document — no abbreviations.
  4. 4
    Notary Signature: Sign your name as it appears on your notary commission — in ink, no stamp or initials.
  5. 5
    Notary Seal: Affix the stamp clearly and entirely within the certificate area. The seal must be legible in a photocopy.
  6. 6
    Commission Expiration: Write or confirm your commission expiration date is shown on or with the seal.
✕
Never correct errors by crossing out and initialing. If you make an error on a certificate before signing it, discard the certificate and complete a new one. Once signed, any error must be noted and a corrected certificate may need to be re-attached — consult management.
7

Journal & Record Keeping

Texas does not mandate a notary journal, but LBS requires one for every notarial act. It is your strongest defense in a dispute.

Required Journal Entry Fields

  1. 1
    Date and time of the act
  2. 2
    Type of notarial act (Acknowledgment / Jurat / Copy Certification)
  3. 3
    Document type (e.g., "Power of Attorney", "Deed of Trust")
  4. 4
    Signer's full name
  5. 5
    Type of ID presented and the ID number (last 4 digits only for security)
  6. 6
    Fee charged
  7. 7
    Signer's signature in the journal
⚠
Journal Security The journal is a legal record. Never leave it unattended on a counter. Store it in a locked drawer when not in use. Do not allow customers to add entries or alter existing ones. Report lost or stolen journals to management and the Texas Secretary of State immediately.

9

Special Services

Witnesses

  1. 1
    Witnesses must also present valid government-issued photo ID — apply the same identity check as for signers.
  2. 2
    Witnesses sign the document and may be asked to sign the journal confirming their presence.
  3. 3
    LBS staff may serve as a witness only when there is no conflict of interest and at least one other staff member is present.
  4. 4
    The notary public may not act as a witness on a document they are also notarizing.

Document Printing

  1. 1
    Print all pages before the customer arrives at the signing area.
  2. 2
    Verify page count before handing the document to the customer.
  3. 3
    Printing fees are separate from notary fees — collect and receipt separately.

Scan to Email

  1. 1
    Confirm the destination email address in writing (have the customer write it down or show it on their phone).
  2. 2
    Scan all notarized pages into a single PDF.
  3. 3
    Send and confirm delivery — ask the customer to check their inbox before leaving.
  4. 4
    Delete the scan file from the shared scanner queue after confirmed delivery.
10

Situations to Decline

Situation Why What to Say
Cannot satisfactorily identify the signer Legal requirement "I'm not able to complete this without an acceptable ID. Please return with one of the listed forms."
Document contains unfilled blank spaces Risk of later falsification "This document has blank fields. Please complete it fully and return."
Signer has already signed a jurat document The oath must precede the signature "For this certificate type, you must sign in my presence after the oath. We'll need to start with a fresh document."
Signer appears under duress or coerced Voluntary act is required Ask to speak privately; if concern persists, decline and offer to call for assistance.
Signer does not appear to understand what they are signing Capacity is required "I need to be confident you understand this document. I'd recommend consulting an attorney first."
You are the signer Self-notarization is prohibited Another commissioned notary must perform the act.
You have a financial interest in the transaction Conflict of interest Disclose the conflict and have another notary perform the act.
Certifying a copy of a vital record (birth, death, marriage) Prohibited by Texas law "Certified copies of vital records must come directly from the issuing agency."
✕
Never feel pressured to proceed. You have the right to decline any notarization for a lawful reason. When in doubt, decline and consult management rather than notarizing and regretting it. An improperly performed notarization is a liability for you personally.
11

End-of-Day Closeout

  • Verify all journal entries for the day are complete — no blank fields, no open lines
  • Lock the notary journal in the designated secure drawer
  • Store the notary stamp/seal in its case, locked away from public access
  • Clear the scanner queue — verify no document files remain on shared drives
  • Confirm all completed appointments are logged in the booking system
  • Note any supplies needing replenishment (ink, certificate forms) for the next shift
Part 2

Corporate Notary Procedures

Applies to appointments booked under an LBS Corporate Account. These clients hold monthly subscription plans (Bronze, Silver, or Gold) and have employees who book through the LBS Corporate Portal. All standard notary requirements (Part 1) still apply — this section covers the additional steps specific to corporate clients.

1

Before the Appointment — Admin Dashboard Review

  1. 1
    Open the LBS Admin Dashboard and navigate to the Appointments tab.
  2. 2
    Locate today's corporate appointments. For each, note:
    • Company name and account code (format: LBS-ACCT-XXXXXX)
    • Appointment code (format: LBS-000001-MMDD-XXXX)
    • Number of signers and documents
    • Special flags: witnesses, printing, scan-to-email
    • Special instructions from the booking
  3. 3
    Check the account's plan tier and current monthly usage — if they are near their limit, be prepared to explain the overage process.
  4. 4
    If printing was requested, print documents before the employee arrives.
ℹ
Plan Tiers at a Glance
Bronze — up to 15 notarial acts/month  ·  Silver — up to 25 acts/month  ·  Gold — up to 100 acts/month.
Overage rate: $10.00 per document + $1.00 per additional stamp beyond the first, when the monthly limit is exceeded.
2

Employee Check-In & Appointment Verification

  1. 1
    Greet the employee and ask for their appointment confirmation code.
  2. 2
    In the Admin Dashboard, locate the appointment by its code and confirm the status shows Scheduled.
  3. 3
    Verify the company name matches what the employee states — they should know what company they are booking for.
  4. 4
    Note any discrepancies (wrong date, cancelled status) before proceeding.
⚠
No Appointment Code = No Service If an employee arrives without a confirmation code and the company cannot provide one promptly, do not proceed. Contact the account's primary contact to verify authorization. Never extend notary services on verbal authorization alone.
✕
Inactive Accounts If the account status shows anything other than Active, do not perform notarial services. Contact management. Do not inform the employee that the account is delinquent — simply state that you need to verify the account and will be in touch with their office.
3

Identity Verification & Document Review

Follow Part 1, Sections 3 and 4 without exception. Corporate account status does not waive any identity or document requirements.

  1. 1
    Check the ID type noted in the booking — if the employee arrives with a different form of ID, confirm it is on the accepted list before proceeding.
  2. 2
    Verify all signers are present — the booking will specify how many. Do not start until all signers are in the room.
  3. 3
    Confirm documents are unsigned (for jurats) or acknowledge the employee is ready to proceed (for acknowledgments).
ℹ
Corporate clients have acknowledged in their enrollment agreement that signers must appear in person, documents must not be signed before arrival, and LBS staff do not provide legal advice or choose certificate types on their behalf.
4

Conducting the Appointment

  1. 1
    Follow standard notarial procedures from Part 1, Sections 5 and 6.
  2. 2
    Handle all services flagged in the booking:
    • Witnesses: Ensure witness is present and has shown ID before beginning.
    • Printing: Should be done before the appointment — if not, print now and verify all pages before beginning.
    • Scan to Email: Have the scan station ready; confirm email address before starting.
  3. 3
    Count the total number of notarial acts performed — this is the figure that goes into the usage tracker.
✓
Count Each Act Separately A single appointment may involve multiple documents and multiple signers. Each individual notarization (per signer, per document) counts as one notarial act against the company's monthly plan.
5

Completing the Appointment in the System

  1. 1
    In the Admin Dashboard, locate the appointment and click Mark Complete.
  2. 2
    The system will automatically increment the account's monthly usage based on the number of documents and stamps recorded in the booking.
  3. 3
    Review the completion confirmation for any overage notification (see Section 6 below).
  4. 4
    Do not collect payment from the employee — all billing goes to the account administrator on file.
⚠
Mark Complete the Same Day. Usage tracking is date-sensitive. Do not leave appointments in "Scheduled" status overnight — mark them complete before your shift ends so monthly totals stay accurate.
6

Overage Billing

Overages occur when the account exceeds its monthly plan limit. The system calculates this automatically when you mark the appointment complete.

  1. 1
    If the completion screen shows an overage charge, note the dollar amount displayed.
  2. 2
    Perform the notarization regardless — the account is still authorized; the overage is a billing matter, not a service denial.
  3. 3
    Do not discuss the overage charge with the employee. The charge will be communicated to the account's billing contact by management.
  4. 4
    If the overage amount appears unusually large, flag it for a manager to review before the billing cycle closes.
ℹ
Overage Rate Reminder
$10.00 per document + $1.00 per additional stamp beyond the first stamp on that document.
Example: 3 documents, 5 stamps total → (3 × $10.00) + (2 × $1.00) = $32.00 overage charge.
7

Account Issues & Escalation

Issue Immediate Action Do Not
Account status is not Active Contact management. Place employee in waiting area. Tell the employee the account is overdue or suspended.
No confirmation code available Call the account's primary contact to obtain authorization in writing. Proceed on a verbal "it should be in the system."
Employee names don't match booking Verify with the primary contact whether the substitution is authorized. Assume the substitution is fine without confirmation.
Employee questions their company's billing Direct them to contact their company's HR or AP department. Discuss the account's balance, plan limit, or overage amounts.
Dispute about services rendered Document the concern, do not attempt to resolve billing independently. Escalate to management by end of day. Issue refunds, adjust invoices, or make promises about credits.
8

Corporate Record Keeping

  1. 1
    Complete a journal entry for every notarial act as required under Part 1, Section 7.
  2. 2
    Include the corporate appointment code in the journal entry's "Notes" field (e.g., LBS-000001-0717-AB12).
  3. 3
    Record the company name alongside the individual signer's name.
  4. 4
    If scan-to-email was performed, note the recipient email address and confirm sent status in the journal.
  5. 5
    Retain any printed copies or originals per the retention policy — do not give the LBS copy to the employee.
✓
Double-check the system before the employee leaves. Confirm the appointment is marked Complete in the dashboard and the usage total has updated. A few seconds now prevents a reconciliation issue at month-end.
